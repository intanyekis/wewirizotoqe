Harry Porterfield’s Television Career

Harry Porterfield’s television career spanned over five decades, leaving an indelible mark on the Chicago broadcasting industry. With his distinctive voice and warm demeanor, he captivated audiences and became a beloved figure in the world of journalism.

51 Years in Chicago Television

For an impressive 51 years, Harry dedicated his life to television in Chicago. He joined CBS 2 in 1964 as a newswriter and quickly rose through the ranks, showcasing his talent as a reporter and anchor. Throughout his career, he became a trusted source of news and a familiar face in households across the city.

Retirement in 2015

In 2015, after a remarkable career, Harry made the difficult decision to retire. His departure from the broadcasting scene marked the end of an era, leaving a void that is still felt today. However, his legacy lives on through the countless stories he shared and the impact he had on the lives of everyday Chicagoans.

Harry Porterfield’s Impact and Legacy

Harry Porterfield’s impact on the field of journalism and the Chicago community is immeasurable. Throughout his career, he left an indelible mark through his unique storytelling and dedication to highlighting the extraordinary stories of everyday people.

“Someone You Should Know” Segment

One of Harry’s most notable contributions was his creation of the “Someone You Should Know” segment. Through this segment, he shed light on the remarkable achievements and inspiring stories of ordinary Chicagoans. Harry’s ability to connect with people and share their stories in a meaningful way resonated with viewers and made a lasting impact on the community.

Boycott and Return to CBS 2

In the mid-80s, Harry faced a challenging period in his career when he was pulled from the anchor desk, leading to a boycott of CBS 2 by the African American community. However, his talent and dedication could not be ignored, and he eventually returned to CBS 2 in 2009. This triumphant comeback showcased not only Harry’s resilience but also the immense respect and admiration he garnered from both his colleagues and the viewers.

Remembering Harry Porterfield

Harry Porterfield’s impact on the world of journalism and the Chicago community will forever be remembered. His remarkable career and dedication to storytelling have left an enduring legacy that continues to inspire and resonate with people today.

Induction into the Silver Circle

In 1998, Harry was honored for his outstanding contributions to television journalism when he was inducted into the Silver Circle of the Chicago chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts and Sciences.
