Mohamed Sanu Bio (Wiki)

Who is Mohamed Sanu?

Mohamed Sanu is a well-recognized American football wide receiver. Sanu plays for the San Francisco 49ers of the National Football League.

Moreover, he played college football at Rutgers and was drafted by the Cincinnati Bengals in the third round of the 2012 NFL Draft. He has also been a member of the Atlanta Falcons, New England Patriots, and Detroit Lions.

Mohamed Sanu- Age, Parents, Siblings, Ethnicity, Education

Mohamed Sanu was born on 22 August 1989. At present, he is 33 years young star. Likewise, his birthplace is New Brunswick, New Jersey, USA.

Mohamed is the son of his father, Samuel Sanu whereas his mother is Aminata Koroma. Moreover, she has a sister and a brother as well.

Talking about his nationality, Sanu holds an American identity whereas his ethnicity is American-African.

Regarding education, the athlete had played football both for his high school and university. He began studying at South Brunswick High School as a triple-option quarterback before enrolling at Rutgers University in 2009. Sanu also studied at Edison High School.

Mohamed Sanu- Professional Life, Careers

Mohamed started playing football when he was studying at South Brunswick High School as a triple-option quarterback. He also led the Vikings to their first playoff berth in nearly 40 years which consist of three rushing touchdowns of at least 80 yards against Edison High School.

Later, at the 2008 NJSIAA Sectional Championships, he placed 2nd in the triple jump and 3rd in the long jump. Sanu also competed as a sprinter making a record of his personal-best times of 6.94seconds in the 55 meters and 24.28 seconds in the 200 meters at the 2008 Merli Invitational.

Talking about his professional career, The Cincinnati Bengals selected Sanu in the third round(83rs overall) of the 2012 NFL Draft, the 12th wide receiver taken in 2012. The athlete then signed a four-year, $2.71 million contract with them that had a signing bonus of $563,252.

Recently, Mohamed was signed to the Detroit Lions practice squad on 6 November 2020. He was elevated to the active roster on November 21 for the team’s Week 11 game against the Carolina Panthers and reverted to the practice squad after the game. Presently, he was re-signed with the 49ers on 30 March 2021.
