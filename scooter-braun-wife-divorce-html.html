Is Scooter Braun Headed for Divorce with Wife Yael? Inside His Married Life

American executive Scooter Braun and his wife of seven years Yael Cohen Braun tread rough waters in their marriage as they reportedly separate temporarily. 

Page Six first reported the news on July 10, 2021, announcing that the pair took a break after months of marital trouble. But it is not clear if they have physically separated. Here's a breakdown of their relationship timeline. 

The Start of Scooter Braun and Wife Yael Cohen's Relationship

The celebrity manager met Cohen when he attended her Ted Talk session about her organization F*** Cancer. Impressed by her initiative, Braun met her in person, and the duo started dating in early 2013. 

Within a year, they got engaged. Braun's famous client Carly Rae Jepsen announced the joyful news through her tweet on January 6, 2014. 

In May 2014, the New York native bought an 11, 137 square feet house with seven bedrooms, a theatre, a swimming pool, and a gym in Los Angeles for $13 million, where his family would later reside.

Scooter Braun and Yael Cohen Married in 2015

Six months after the engagement, Braun and Cohen tied the knot through a star-studded wedding ceremony in Whistler, British Columbia, Canada, on July 6, 2014.

Celebrities like Tom Hanks, Ed Sheeran, Sophia Bush, Asher Roth, and Braun's clients Justin Bieber and Carly Rae Jepsen attended the ceremony. 

Making the day more special, Bieber performed his version of 'All You Need Is Love' and teamed up with Jepsen for 'Call Me Maybe.' 

After the wedding, the couple welcomed three kids into their family. They gave birth to their first child, a boy Jagger Joseph, on February 6, 2015.

Their second son Levi Magnus arrived in the world on December 2, 2016. Following a two-year gap, they finally welcomed their third child and only daughter, Hart Violet, on December 1, 2018. 

Will Scooter Braun and Yael Cohen Divorce?

Braun and Cohen are trying their best to save their marriage, TMZ confirmed. Though the issues in their marriage surfaced out, there has been no confirmation if they've physically moved out from their home.

Also, neither of the party has mentioned divorce. Instead, the pair is apparently trying to work things out, posting pictures on social media and keeping up the happy family image. 

On their 7th wedding anniversary, Braun, who manages Ariana Grande and Demi Lovato, took to Instagram to pour his gratitude for his mining heiress wife. Posting a picture from their wedding, he thanked her for helping him grow and be the best version of himself. 

Shedding light on the positivity, the 40-year-old mentioned how their adventure as a married couple was only beginning. His cancer activist wife replied to the post with a brief message, "A Team 4lyfe," and hinted that their marriage still had hope.

Friends and family, including Kevin Mazur, Jay Shetty, Kenny Hamilton, and Rita Wilson, wished happiness and strength for the couple in the comment section.
