Michael Dante DiMartino Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

Michael Dante DiMartino is an American animator best known for co-creating, executive producing, and storey editing the Nickelodeon animated television series Avatar: The Last Airbender and The Legend of Korra. He was born in the Vermont town of Shelburne. Bryan Konietzko, with whom he co-created Avatar, was his professor at the Rhode Island School of Design. Mike worked at Film Roman for twelve years before to Avatar, assisting in the production of King of the Hill, Family Guy, and Mission Hill, as well as his own animated short, Atomic Love, which was shown at a number of prestigious film festivals.

 The last episode of Avatar: The Last Airbender features a tribute to his father’s memories. In a 2010 interview, Nickelodeon’s president, Cyma Zarghami, revealed that Michael Dante DiMartino and Bryan Konietzko, co-creator of Avatar: The Last Airbender, were working on a new series for the network named The Legend of Korra. The series began on April 14, 2012, with 12 episodes for the first book “Air” and 14 for the second book “Spirits,” which aired to 2.60 million viewers in the United States on September 13, 2013. Nickelodeon approved additional 26 episodes, split into two 13-episode books, in response to tremendous public demand (Book 3 and Book 4).
