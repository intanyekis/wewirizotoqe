Ann Pugh Obituary, Funeral Details And Visitation, Family Mourns

0SHARESShareTweet

Ann Pugh Obituary, Death – On Tuesday, February 6, 2024, Winchester, VA, and the world lost a remarkable soul as Ann Marie Pugh, aged 95, passed away peacefully at the home of her daughter, surrounded by her loving family. Born on August 17, 1928, in Reynolds Store, VA, Ann Marie’s life was a testament to love, faith, and the enduring bond of family.

Early Years and Faithful Beginnings: A Daughter of Reynolds Store

Ann Marie, the daughter of Robert E. and Bessie Braithwaite Patterson, began her journey in the close-knit community of Reynolds Store, VA. Her upbringing laid the foundation for a life dedicated to service, faith, and the joy found in simple pleasures. She found her spiritual home at Mt. Olive United Methodist Church, where she actively participated in the ladies church group, affectionately known as the “Olive Oyl’s.”

Educational Service and Kindergarten Aide: Nurturing Young Minds

With a heart for education, Ann Marie contributed over 20 years of her life as a Kindergarten Aide at Gainesboro & Indian Hollow Elementary Schools. Her commitment to nurturing young minds left an indelible mark on the hearts of generations to come. The halls of these schools echoed with her warmth, kindness, and dedication to fostering a love for learning.

Passions and Hobbies: A Garden of Joy and Creative Pursuits

Outside the educational realm, Ann Marie found solace and joy in her various passions and hobbies. Her green thumb transformed her flower bed into a vibrant garden, where blooms mirrored the beauty of her spirit. Sewing, crafting, and a love for audiobooks filled her days with creativity and imagination. Decorating for holidays, especially Christmas, became a cherished tradition that brought joy to her family and the community.

Cherished Moments with Family: A Grandmother’s Love

For Ann Marie, the true essence of life rested in the love shared with her family. She leaves behind a legacy of three devoted daughters: Cheryl Knight (Rick) of Cross Junction, VA; Tina Evans (John) of Hayfield, VA; Julie Michael (Allen) of Mt. Williams, VA. Her two grandchildren, Matthew Knight (Angela) and Molly Knight (Zach), became the recipients of her boundless affection. In addition, two step-grandchildren, Kaley and Joshua Evans, and three great-grandchildren, Maeve Knight, Ava, and Abby James, enriched her life with laughter, love, and cherished memories.

Remembering Loved Ones: A Family United in Grief

Ann Marie is preceded in death by her brother, Walter Patterson, and sister, Elsie Dolan. As family and friends come together to celebrate her life, they find solace in the memories shared and the impact she had on each of their lives. The bond formed by love transcends the boundaries of time, and Ann Marie’s legacy lives on in the hearts of those who had the privilege of knowing her.

A Final Farewell: Honoring Ann Marie’s Memory

A funeral service will be held at the Giffin Funeral Home & Crematory in Capon Bridge, WV on Friday, February 16, 2024, at 12:00 PM. Pastor Kathleen Haynes will officiate, offering words of comfort and reflection. Inurnment will take place at a later date in Woodlawn Cemetery near Cross Junction, VA. Friends and well-wishers are invited to pay their respects at the funeral home one hour prior to the service.

As the community mourns the loss of Ann Marie Pugh, they find strength in the shared memories and the enduring impact of a life well-lived. Her spirit will forever resonate in the hearts of those who had the privilege of being part of her journey. May she rest in eternal peace, surrounded by the love that defined her remarkable life.
